Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about studying abroad

What are the benefits of studying in India for international students?

India offers numerous benefits for international students including:

  • World-class education at affordable costs
  • Diverse range of courses and programs
  • Cultural diversity and rich heritage
  • Opportunities for research and innovation
  • Growing economy with career opportunities
  • English as the medium of instruction in most universities
What are the entry requirements for studying in India?

Entry requirements vary by university and program, but generally include:

  • Completed secondary education with minimum grades
  • Proof of English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL) for non-native speakers
  • Valid passport and student visa
  • Financial proof for tuition and living expenses
  • Medical insurance coverage
  • Specific entrance exams for certain programs (like JEE for engineering)
How much does it cost to study in India as an international student?

The cost of studying in India varies depending on the university and program:

  • Tuition fees: $2,000 - $8,000 per year for most programs
  • Living expenses: $3,000 - $5,000 per year
  • Medical programs may cost $10,000 - $20,000 per year
  • Many universities offer scholarships for international students
  • Part-time work opportunities are available to offset costs
What is the process for obtaining a student visa for India?

The student visa process for India involves:

  • Receiving an admission letter from a recognized Indian institution
  • Completing the online visa application form
  • Submitting required documents (passport, photos, financial proof, etc.)
  • Paying the visa application fee
  • Attending a visa interview if required
  • Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks
Can international students work while studying in India?

Yes, international students in India can work under certain conditions:

  • Up to 20 hours per week during academic sessions
  • Full-time during scheduled breaks
  • Work must be on-campus or related to the field of study
  • Permission from the institution and relevant authorities is required
  • Some restrictions apply depending on the type of visa
